Polling Average


Now, you can't have a working model without a functional polling average, so I'm starting this page in the inner workings of the model's inputs. The polling average among my provincial models covers every poll throughout the term. So my Quebec model, for instance, covers the polls done since the 2022 election, which you can see on the Wikipedia page for the upcoming Quebec election.

*important note, when not in a writ period, to fix polls from just a couple of months ago from being weighted next to nothing, the W value follows a pattern being multiplied by a higher amount per previous year:
2024 - multiplied by 5
 2023 - multiplied by 10
 2022 - multiplied by 15
 2021 - multiplied by 20

So, getting to how the formula actually works, first you need to plug in all our valuables, which are listed here:
W - weighting
R - rating (as determined by 338canada, where an A+ is a 7, going down half a point to below a D- or if a firm is unlisted, where they get a 1)
n - sample size of the poll
d - date (the number of days from the poll to today)
ΣE - error among all the parties
xᵢ - the result of the current poll for a respective party
x̄ - the current average for the same party leading up to that poll
