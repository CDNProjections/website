Polling Average


Now, you can't have a working model without a functional polling average, so I'm starting this page in the inner workings of the model's inputs. The polling average among my provincial models covers every poll throughout the term. So my Quebec model, for instance, covers the polls done since the 2022 election, which you can see on the Wikipedia page for the upcoming Quebec election. However, this is only different on the federal level, which has a rolling polling average covering the previous three months of polls, both to keep things frequently updated, and so that the average doesn't include ancient polls that'd take weeks to input all the respective data for. After the April Federal Election, I plan on starting new and incorporating the federal polls from the next full term to align with the provincial systems.

*important note, when not in a writ period, to fix polls from just a couple of months ago from being weighted next to nothing, the W value follows a pattern being multiplied by a higher amount per previous year:
2024 - multiplied by 10
 2023 - multiplied by 25
 2022 - multiplied by 40
 2021 - multiplied by 55

So, getting to how the formula actually works, first you need to plug in all our valuables, which are listed here: W - weighting
R - rating (as determined by 338canada, where an A+ is a 7, going down half a point to below a D- or if a firm is unlisted, where they get a 1)
n - sample size of the poll d - date (the number of days from the poll to today) ΣE - error among all the parties xᵢ - the result of the current poll for a respective party x̄ - the current average for the same party leading up to that poll ...and I think it's probably easiest to do an example to fill out this formula.
